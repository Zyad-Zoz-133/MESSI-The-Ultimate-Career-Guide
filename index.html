Messi's Historic Journey (Season by Season)

Season 2004/2005 : The first Professional Season

In the 2004/2005 season, a 17-year-old Lionel Messi made his official debut for Barcelona's first team.
He became the youngest player to ever play and score for the club in La Liga at that time, after scoring his iconic first professional goal against Albacete with a beautiful lob assist from Ronaldinho.
This was the magical start of his legendary career.

2004/2005 season stats

  • Matches Played: 9 matches
  • Goals Scored: 1 goal
  • Assists: 0 assists
  • Trophies Won: La Liga Title (2004/2005)

Season 2005/2006 : The First UEFA Champions League

In the 2005/2006 season, Messi established himself as a regular starter for Barcelona.
He amazed Europe with a legendary performance against Chelsea at Stamford Bridge in the UEFA Champions League.
Although a thigh injury cut his season short in March, he played a vital role in Barcelona's domestic and European dominance.

2005/2006 season stats

  • Matches Played: 25 matches
  • Goals Scored: 8 goal
  • Assists: 5 assists
  • Trophies Won: UEFA Champions League, La Liga Title, Supercopa de España

Season 2006-2007: The El Clásico Hat-Trick

This was the breakthrough season where Messi proved he was a superstar.
He became the first player in 12 years to score a hat-trick in El Clásico against Real Madrid, securing a 3-3 draw for Barcelona.
He also scored a legendary goal against Getafe that was almost identical to Diego Maradona's famous "Goal of the Century.

2006/2007 season stats

  • Matches Played: 36 matches
  • Goals Scored: 17 goal
  • Assists: 3 assists
  • Trophies Won: Supercopa de España

Season 2007/2008: Rising to the Top

In this season, Messi established himself as one of the best players in the world, finishing third in the Ballon d'Or and second in the FIFA World Player of the Year awards.
He became Barcelona's main driving force in attack, showing incredible dribbling skills and consistency, although the team suffered from injuries and missed out on major trophies.

2007/2008 season stats

  • Matches Played: 40 matches
  • Goals Scored: 16 goal
  • Assists: 16 assists
  • Trophies Won: None

Season 2008/2009: The Historic Sextuple

This was the historic season where Messi inherited the number 10 shirt after Ronaldinho's departure and thrived under coach Pep Guardiola.
Messi became the superstar of one of the greatest football teams in history, scoring a crucial header in the Champions League final against Manchester United.
Barcelona won every single tournament they played in, completing a historic sextuple.

2008/2009 season stats

  • Matches Played: 51 matches
  • Goals Scored: 38 goal
  • Assists: 19 assists
  • Trophies Won:
    • UEFA Champions League
    • La Liga
    • Copa del Rey
    • UEFA Super Cup
    • FIFA Club World Cup
    • Supercopa de España
  • Individual Awards: First Ballon d'Or award

Season 2009/2010: The Golden Boot Dominance

Messi continued his unbelievable form by scoring 47 goals in all competitions, equaling Ronaldo's club record from 1996/97.
He scored four goals in a single Champions League match against Arsenal and led Barcelona to retain the La Liga title with a record-breaking 99 points, earning his first European Golden Shoe.

2009/2010 season stats

  • Matches Played: 53 matches
  • Goals Scored: 47 goal
  • Assists: 12 assists
  • Trophies Won:
    • La Liga
    • FIFA Club World Cup
    • Supercopa de España
  • Individual Awards: Ballon d'Or (Second consecutive), European Golden Shoe

Season 2010/2011: The Wembley Masterclass

One of Messi's greatest seasons in football history.
He scored 53 goals and provided 25 assists, leading Barcelona to a historic La Liga and Champions League double.
His performance in the Champions League final at Wembley against Manchester United was unforgettable, where he scored a brilliant long-range goal and was named Man of the Match.

2010/2011 season stats

  • Matches Played: 55 matches
  • Goals Scored: 53 goal
  • Assists: 25 assists
  • Trophies Won:
    • UEFA Champions league
    • La Liga
    • FIFA Club World Cup
    • Supercopa de España
  • Ballon d'Or (Third consecutive)

Season 2011/2012: The Year of 91 Goals

This was the ultimate goal-scoring season in football history.
Messi broke the all-time world record for most goals scored in a single calendar year, netting an unbelievable 91 goals in 2012.
He also became Barcelona's all-time top scorer at just 24 years old and scored five goals in a single Champions League match against Bayer Leverkusen.

2011/2012 season stats

  • Matches Played: 60 matches
  • Goals Scored: 73 goal
  • Assists: 29 assists
  • Trophies Won:
    • Copa del Rey
    • FIFA Club World Cup
    • Supercopa de España
  • Individual Awards: Ballon d'Or (Fourth consecutive), European Golden Shoe

Season 2012/2013: The 100-Point Champions

Despite suffering from muscle injuries towards the end of the season, Messi led Barcelona to reclaim the La Liga title with a historic record of 100 points.
He achieved a breathtaking football record by scoring in 21 consecutive league matches against every single opponent in the tournament.

2012/2013 season stats

  • Matches Played: 50 matches
  • Goals Scored: 60 goal
  • Assists: 16 assists
  • Trophies Won:
    • La Liga
  • Individual Awards:European Golden Shoe

Season 2013/2014: The Transition & World Cup Heartbreak

This was a challenging transition season for Messi and Barcelona under coach Gerardo "Tata" Martino.
Messi suffered from several hamstring injuries that kept him out for weeks, and Barcelona missed out on major trophies at the very end of the season.
However, he still managed to become Barcelona's all-time top scorer in all competitions.

2013/2014 season stats

  • Matches Played: 46 matches
  • Goals Scored: 41 goal
  • Assists: 14 assists
  • Trophies Won:
    • Supercopa de España
  • Individual Awards: World Cup 2014 Golden Ball Shoe
