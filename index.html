Messi's Historic Journey (Season by Season)

Season 2004/2005 : The first Professional Season (17y)

In the 2004/2005 season, a 17-year-old Lionel Messi made his official debut for Barcelona's first team.
He became the youngest player to ever play and score for the club in La Liga at that time, after scoring his iconic first professional goal against Albacete with a beautiful lob assist from Ronaldinho.
This was the magical start of his legendary career.

2004/2005 season stats

  • Matches Played: 9 matches
  • Goals Scored: 1 goal
  • Assists: 0 assists
  • Trophies Won: La Liga Title (2004/2005)

Season 2005/2006 : The First UEFA Champions League (18y)

In the 2005/2006 season, Messi established himself as a regular starter for Barcelona.
He amazed Europe with a legendary performance against Chelsea at Stamford Bridge in the UEFA Champions League.
Although a thigh injury cut his season short in March, he played a vital role in Barcelona's domestic and European dominance.

2005/2006 season stats

  • Matches Played: 25 matches
  • Goals Scored: 8 goal
  • Assists: 5 assists
  • Trophies Won: UEFA Champions League, La Liga Title, Supercopa de España

Season 2006-2007: The El Clásico Hat-Trick (19y)

This was the breakthrough season where Messi proved he was a superstar.
He became the first player in 12 years to score a hat-trick in El Clásico against Real Madrid, securing a 3-3 draw for Barcelona.
He also scored a legendary goal against Getafe that was almost identical to Diego Maradona's famous "Goal of the Century.

2006/2007 season stats

  • Matches Played: 36 matches
  • Goals Scored: 17 goal
  • Assists: 3 assists
  • Trophies Won: Supercopa de España

Season 2007/2008: Rising to the Top (20y)

In this season, Messi established himself as one of the best players in the world, finishing third in the Ballon d'Or and second in the FIFA World Player of the Year awards.
He became Barcelona's main driving force in attack, showing incredible dribbling skills and consistency, although the team suffered from injuries and missed out on major trophies.

2007/2008 season stats

  • Matches Played: 40 matches
  • Goals Scored: 16 goal
  • Assists: 16 assists
  • Trophies Won: None

Season 2008/2009: The Historic Sextuple (21y)

This was the historic season where Messi inherited the number 10 shirt after Ronaldinho's departure and thrived under coach Pep Guardiola.
Messi became the superstar of one of the greatest football teams in history, scoring a crucial header in the Champions League final against Manchester United.
Barcelona won every single tournament they played in, completing a historic sextuple.

2008/2009 season stats

  • Matches Played: 51 matches
  • Goals Scored: 38 goal
  • Assists: 19 assists
  • Trophies Won:
    • UEFA Champions League
    • La Liga
    • Copa del Rey
    • UEFA Super Cup
    • FIFA Club World Cup
    • Supercopa de España
  • Individual Awards: First Ballon d'Or award

Season 2009/2010: The Golden Boot Dominance (22y)

Messi continued his unbelievable form by scoring 47 goals in all competitions, equaling Ronaldo's club record from 1996/97.
He scored four goals in a single Champions League match against Arsenal and led Barcelona to retain the La Liga title with a record-breaking 99 points, earning his first European Golden Shoe.

2009/2010 season stats

  • Matches Played: 53 matches
  • Goals Scored: 47 goal
  • Assists: 12 assists
  • Trophies Won:
    • La Liga
    • FIFA Club World Cup
    • Supercopa de España
  • Individual Awards: Ballon d'Or (Second consecutive), European Golden Shoe

Season 2010/2011: The Wembley Masterclass (23y)

One of Messi's greatest seasons in football history.
He scored 53 goals and provided 25 assists, leading Barcelona to a historic La Liga and Champions League double.
His performance in the Champions League final at Wembley against Manchester United was unforgettable, where he scored a brilliant long-range goal and was named Man of the Match.

2010/2011 season stats

  • Matches Played: 55 matches
  • Goals Scored: 53 goal
  • Assists: 25 assists
  • Trophies Won:
    • UEFA Champions league
    • La Liga
    • FIFA Club World Cup
    • Supercopa de España
  • Ballon d'Or (Third consecutive)

Season 2011/2012: The Year of 91 Goals (24y)

This was the ultimate goal-scoring season in football history.
Messi broke the all-time world record for most goals scored in a single calendar year, netting an unbelievable 91 goals in 2012.
He also became Barcelona's all-time top scorer at just 24 years old and scored five goals in a single Champions League match against Bayer Leverkusen.

2011/2012 season stats

  • Matches Played: 60 matches
  • Goals Scored: 73 goal
  • Assists: 29 assists
  • Trophies Won:
    • Copa del Rey
    • FIFA Club World Cup
    • Supercopa de España
  • Individual Awards: Ballon d'Or (Fourth consecutive), European Golden Shoe

Season 2012/2013: The 100-Point Champions (25y)

Despite suffering from muscle injuries towards the end of the season, Messi led Barcelona to reclaim the La Liga title with a historic record of 100 points.
He achieved a breathtaking football record by scoring in 21 consecutive league matches against every single opponent in the tournament.

2012/2013 season stats

  • Matches Played: 50 matches
  • Goals Scored: 60 goal
  • Assists: 16 assists
  • Trophies Won:
    • La Liga
  • Individual Awards:European Golden Shoe

Season 2013/2014: The Transition & World Cup Heartbreak (26y)

This was a challenging transition season for Messi and Barcelona under coach Gerardo "Tata" Martino.
Messi suffered from several hamstring injuries that kept him out for weeks, and Barcelona missed out on major trophies at the very end of the season.
However, he still managed to become Barcelona's all-time top scorer in all competitions.

2013/2014 season stats

  • Matches Played: 46 matches
  • Goals Scored: 41 goal
  • Assists: 14 assists
  • Trophies Won:
    • Supercopa de España
  • Individual Awards: World Cup 2014 Golden Ball Shoe

Season 2014/2015: The Treble & MSN Dominance (27y)

This was one of the greatest seasons in Messi’s entire career.
Under new manager Luis Enrique, Messi moved back to the right wing to form the legendary "MSN" trio alongside Luis Suárez and Neymar.
Messi was completely unstoppable, shaking off the previous year's disappointment to lead Barcelona to their historic second continental Treble.
His iconic dribble against Boateng and goal against Bayern Munich became defining moments of football history.

2014/2015 season stats

  • Matches Played: 57 matches
  • Goals Scored: 58 goal
  • Assists: 31 assists
  • Trophies Won:(The Treble)
    • La Liga
    • Copa del Rey
    • UEFA Champions League
  • Individual Awards: Ballon d'Or (5th Ballon d'Or), UEFA Best Player in Europe

Season 2015/2016: The Domestic Double & FIFA Club World Cup (28y)

Following the incredible Treble, Messi continued his historic partnership with Suárez and Neymar.
Despite suffering a knee injury that kept him out for two months, Messi returned stronger than ever, adapting his game to become more of a playmaker while still scoring crucial goals.
He helped Barcelona dominate domestic football once again, securing consecutive La Liga and Copa del Rey titles, alongside global glory in Japan.

2015/2016 season stats

  • Matches Played: 49 matches
  • Goals Scored: 41 goal
  • Assists: 26 assists
  • Trophies Won:
    • La Liga
    • Copa del Rey
    • UEFA Super Cup
    • FIFA Club World Cup
  • Individual Awards: La Liga Top Assist Provider, Copa del Rey Top Scorer

Season 2016/2017: The Golden Shoe & El Clásico Drama (29y)

This season was a showcase of Messi’s individual brilliance and lethal goal-scoring form.
Despite Barcelona missing out on the league title and Champions League, Messi carried the team on his back with unforgettable performances.
The absolute peak of the season came at the Santiago Bernabéu, where he scored a dramatic 92nd-minute winner against Real Madrid—marking his 500th official goal for Barcelona—and celebrated by famously holding his shirt up to the crowd.

2016/2017 season stats

  • Matches Played: 52 matches

  • Trophies Won:
    • Supercopa de España
    • Copa del Rey
  • Individual Awards: European Golden Shoe , Pichichi Trophy

Season 2017/2018: Domination in Spain (30y)

Following the shocking departure of Neymar, Barcelona entered a new era under manager Ernesto Valverde.
Messi took on even more responsibility, adapting to a tactical system that relied heavily on his creative genius.
He proved to be absolutely unstoppable in domestic competitions, guiding Barcelona to a near-invincible La Liga campaign (losing only one match all season) and securing another domestic Double.

2017/2018 season stats

  • Matches Played: 54 matches
  • Goals Scored: 45 goal

    • Copa del Rey

Season 2018/2019: The Captaincy & One-Man Show (31y)

This season marked Messi’s first official year as Barcelona’s first captain following Andrés Iniesta's departure.
He promised the fans at the Camp Nou to do everything to bring the Champions League trophy back, and he put on an absolute masterclass to try and make it happen.
Although the season ended with a heartbreaking Champions League exit at Anfield, Messi's individual performances were sublime—including a legendary 35-yard free-kick against Liverpool and spectacular hat-tricks that earned him a historic sixth Ballon d'Or.

2018/2019 season stats

  • Matches Played: 50 matches
  • Goals Scored: 51 goal
  • Assists: 22 assists
  • Trophies Won:
    • La Liga
    • Supercopa de España
  • Individual Awards: 2019 Ballon d'Or (6th Ballon d'Or), European Golden Shoe, Pichichi Trophy, UEFA Champions League Top Scorer

Season 2019/2020: The Pandemic Season & Milestone 700 (32y)

This was a chaotic and unprecedented season, heavily disrupted by the COVID-19 pandemic and a three-month football lockdown.
Off the pitch, tension grew between Messi and the club's board, while on the pitch, Messi had to carry an unstable Barcelona squad.
Despite the team finishing trophyless and suffering a humiliating 8-2 defeat against Bayern Munich in the Champions League, Messi made history individually by reaching his 700th career goal and breaking the La Liga record for the most assists in a single season.

2019/2020 season stats

  • Matches Played: 44 matches
  • Goals Scored: 31 goal
  • Assists: 27 assists (La Liga Record)
  • Trophies Won:None
  • Individual Awards: Pichichi Trophy, Laureus World Sportsman of the Year
